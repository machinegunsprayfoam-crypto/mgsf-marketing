Why steel buildings sweat — and how foam stops it

A metal building is a giant cold surface. Warm, humid air inside hits that cold steel and condenses — the same way a cold glass sweats on a summer day. That moisture drips onto your equipment, rusts fasteners, and ruins whatever's stored below. Fiberglass batts and vinyl-backed blanket insulation slow heat but let the air keep reaching the metal, so the sweating never really stops. Closed-cell spray foam bonds directly to the panel as one continuous air and vapor barrier — the warm air can't touch the cold steel, so the condensation stops at the source.

🚫 Stops the sweat

💪 R-value that stays put

Common questions

Why does my metal building sweat?

Bare steel is cold. When warm, humid inside air touches it, the moisture condenses and drips — like a cold glass sweating in summer. Closed-cell foam bonds to the steel as an air and vapor barrier so the warm air never reaches the cold panel, stopping the sweating at the source.

What R-value can I get in a steel building?

Closed-cell foam delivers about R-6.5 per inch, so a few inches gives real R-value plus a full air seal — far more effective per inch than batts or blanket, and it won't sag or trap moisture against the metal.

Can you spray an existing metal building?

Usually yes — we spray directly to clean steel or over framing after prep. We'll assess your building and give you a straight answer on prep and coverage.

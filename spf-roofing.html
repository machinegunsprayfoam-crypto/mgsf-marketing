Why SPF roofing?

Spray polyurethane foam roofing goes down as a single seamless layer over your existing low-slope roof — no seams or fasteners for water to find. It adds real insulation value on top of stopping leaks, and a protective coating shields it from UV. When it ages, you recoat it instead of tearing it off — so it can be renewed for decades.

🚫 Stops leaks

Seamless foam self-flashes around penetrations, curbs and seams — the spots where built-up and membrane roofs usually fail.

🌡️ Adds insulation

You're insulating and re-roofing in one step — lower energy bills on shops, warehouses and ag buildings.

♻️ Recoatable

Recoat at the end of the coating's life instead of a full tear-off — a renewable roof, not a landfill.

Coatings that make it last

We finish every SPF roof with a protective coating — acrylic or silicone depending on your roof and climate — applied to manufacturer spec. Silicone stands up to ponding water and UV on flat roofs; acrylic is a cost-effective, reflective option. The coating is what you renew down the road to keep the roof going.

Common questions

How long does an SPF roof last?

The foam itself is long-lived; the protective coating is the wear layer. Recoat it at the end of the coating's service life and the roof keeps going — often for decades. Call for specifics on your roof.

Can you spray over my existing roof?

Often yes — SPF can go over many existing low-slope roofs after prep, which saves the cost and downtime of a full tear-off. We'll assess yours and tell you straight.

Do you do metal and ag building roofs?

Yes — sealing leaking seams and fasteners on metal, shop and ag roofs is one of the best uses for SPF. It's a big part of what we do in this region.

Why spray foam?

Spray foam does what batts and blown-in can't: it seals the air leaks that drive your heating and cooling bills, and it adds real R-value at the same time. In our climate — long, cold winters and hard wind — that air seal is the difference between a building you fight all winter and one that just holds temperature.

🌡️ Seals air leaks

Foam expands into every gap and seam, stopping the drafts that steal your heat. One step, air barrier and insulation together.

💪 High R-value

Closed-cell delivers about R-6.5 per inch and adds structural rigidity — ideal for metal buildings, crawlspaces and roofs.

💧 Controls moisture

Closed-cell foam acts as an air and vapor barrier, cutting condensation and helping protect against moisture and rot.

What we spray

Closed-Cell Foam

Dense, high-R, air + vapor barrier. Best for metal buildings, shops, crawlspaces, rim joists, roofs and anywhere moisture control matters.

Open-Cell Foam

Great expansion and sound control for interior walls and large attics where a vapor-permeable insulation is the right call.

SPF Roofing

Seamless spray-foam roofing with a protective coating — stops leaks and adds insulation on low-slope commercial and ag roofs. See SPF roofing →

Protective Coatings

Common questions

Closed-cell or open-cell — which do I need?

Closed-cell for metal buildings, crawlspaces, roofs and anywhere moisture and high R-value matter. Open-cell for interior walls and large attics where sound control and a vapor-permeable foam are the right fit. We'll tell you straight which one your job calls for.

Do you do metal buildings and shops?

Yes — pole barns, shops, and steel buildings are a big part of what we do. Closed-cell foam seals the metal, stops condensation, and holds temperature.
